Mindful Scroll: An Infinite Scroll Abstract Colouring App for Mindfulness

Saralin Zassman, Craig S. Kaplan, and Daniel Vogel

PDF Mobile App
Social media apps like TikTok and Instagram are designed to capture our attention and keep us glued to our screens. Mindful Scroll is a mobile colouring app that uses infinite scrolling to cultivate an uninterrupted state of flow and relaxation.

Quick Facts

  1. Our app enhances mindfulness over time, demonstrating comparable results to traditional colouring and existing mindfulness apps.
  2. Increasing engagement can actually improve the effectiveness of digital mindfulness practices.
  3. Colouring acts as a distraction and helps to alleviate the pressure of traditional mindfulness practices, where maintaining self-directed focus is often expected.

Abstract

We design and evaluate Mindful Scroll, a mobile application for mindfulness that encourages a slow and deliberate approach to colouring. The app renders an infinite scroll of generated geometric tilings that reveal pseudo-random colour palettes and fill effects when coloured using a finger or pen. A five-day study (N=28) evaluated the efficacy of the app in reducing anxiety and enhancing mindfulness. The results indicate that the app is capable of promoting a greater sense of mindfulness over time and produced similar results across several measures compared to traditional structured colouring and existing mindfulness-based mobile applications. All participants expressed a desire to use the app again, with a majority stating they felt more mindful after the study.

Mindful Scroll

    Mindful Scroll is a mobile colouring app for mindfulness that uses infinite scrolling to cultivate an uninterrupted state of flow and relaxation.

    As users scroll, the app continuously generates pseudorandom geometric tilings, with distinct colour palettes and fill styles that are revealed by colouring. To encourage a slow and purposeful approach to colouring, stroke widths decrease when colouring too fast, and increase when colouring slowly. Similarly, the soundscape volume increases when colouring slowly and decreases if too fast or outside the lines.

    When scrolling up, previous drawings look as though they gradually disappear. This redirects attention towards the act of colouring and not the eventual outcome.

    To investigate how different levels of engagement influence the practice of mindfulness, we created two versions of the app: an enhanced version with more effects and variation, and a simpler version inspired by the minimal approach of the Pause app.

Experiment

We conducted a five-day study to evaluate the impact of the app on mindfulness. The study adopts much of the design used to evaluate Pause, using attention, overall well-being, mood, and happiness as measures of mindfulness. Two additional metrics, state mindfulness and state anxiety, were introduced to enable a relative meta-comparison with traditional colouring. Additionally, a semi-structured interview was conducted after the experiment to gather qualitative feedback.

Our results indicate that Mindful Scroll is an effective tool for cultivating mindfulness. Across several measures, the enhanced version was more adept at increasing mindfulness than our simple version. It was the only app that made participants feel happier over time, and it also had more of a positive impact on mood. Even with its increased stimulation, the app remained equally effective in reducing anxiety and improving attention. It strikes a balance between simplicity and engagement, one that we believe is lacking in the current market of MBMAs.

We hope our work inspires the development of mindfulness apps that provide engaging and visually immersive user experiences.
